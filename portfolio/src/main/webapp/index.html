About Me:

My name is Kristen Mason, and I'm a rising junior at Scripps College in Los Angeles, where I study computer science. I've been working from home in Saint Louis, Missouri, where I spend my free time baking, reading the "newest" Game of Thrones novel, watching Youtube videos, binging more Netflix shows than I'd ever care to admit, and playing with my puppy, Fitz.

Experience:

Here is a brief snapshot of my work and extracurricular experiences over the years, in a less formal setting than a traditional resume.

Grader/Tutor for Introduction to Computer Science

Hands down my favorite on-campus job! I get to tutor students taking Harvey Mudd College's introductory CS course, CS5, and get to help grade their exams and homework. We're called "grutors", short for Grader/Tutor, not group tutor like I originally thought. In the fall, over 300 students are enrolled in CS5, and every Thursday, Professor Dodds will host grading dinners in an auditorium. The CS department buys dinner for all of the grutors so we can grade while eating and listening to music. My favorite dinner is Chinese food from Star Wok, and since I sit at the front of the auditorium, I love to try and talk Prof. Dodds into playing weird music (my go-tos genres are Christmas and Disney.)

Lifeguard at the Scripps Pool

Even though I won't be getting recertified for this job in the fall, I had a lot of fun working as a lifeguard. I mean, just check out the pool! Who wouldn't love working at such a beautiful place? I actually took that picture while on one of my shifts (but don't tell my manager that!) As a rule, lifeguards can only be on duty for 15 minutes at at time, so half of my shift would be dedicated to homework time! This was especially nice during colder months, when no patrons would show up during my shift, so myself and the other guard on duty could watch Netflix together!

Summer Intern at The Intelligence Factory

I spent the summer working at this small startup with one of my friends from high school. I was in charge of managing the company's Wordpress site. It was a lot of fun until one day the site went down during server maintenance right when I clicked publish changes, and I spent 20 minutes convinced that I'd accidentally deleted the company's website off of the internet! Other than that, I worked a lot with Google Analytics to get insight's into the company's SEO and take steps to improve it.

Research Intern at WashU

Working at WashU was a blast. I spent two summers working at their NASA's Planetary Data Science Geosciences node in the remote sensing lab, after spending my senior service project volunteering there. Some of the coolest things I got to do while working there were to sit in on a meeting between JPL and Johns Hopkins University as they planned out specifics for the Dragonfly Mission to Titan in 2026(!), wear a VR headset that allowed me to walk around on the surface of Mars, work with data that came directely from both the Curiousity Rover and the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter, watch my coworker defend her dissertation and receive her doctorate, and run data processing directley through NASA's supercomputer, Pleiades.

Extracurriculars

On campus, I'm the treasurer for my school's women in computer science club, the Scripps Code. I'm the floor captain and outside hitter for the Claremont College's women club volleyball team, and last year we ranked 3rd in the SCCVBL, the only D3 school to make it that far. Our school's hackathon was cancelled this year, but my freshman year my team won 1st place in our division, and now we participate in human centered design competitions such as the Sparkathon or IdeaJam hosted by Intuit.

Travel Blog

Here are some of my favorite places I've visited in the past few years!

Australia

This past winter, my family went to Australia for New Years. We visited Sydney, Adelaide, Melbourne, and Hamilton Island. My favorite experiences from this trip were the fireworks on New Year's Eve in Sydney Harbour, and going scuba diving on the Great Barrier Reef! Such a cool thing to check off the bucketlist! We actually visited during the awful fires they had at the beginning of the year. You can see in the picture of the Opera House that everything is smoky and muted. During our stay in Adelaide I actually woke up one morning to the smell of burning rubber because the fires were so close!
